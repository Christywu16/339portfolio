Welcome to My To-Read Booklist

The Moon and Sixpence
by W. Somerset Maugham


Summary: In the first person, through the description of the heart of the central character Charles Strickland, it shows how a middle-aged British stockbroker suddenly left his wife and children to pursue his dream of becoming an artist. The first story is based on the life of the painter Paul Gauguin.

Guns, Germs, and Steel: The Fates of Human Societies
by Jared Diamond


Summary: According to the author, this book attempts to provide "a brief history of all people in the last 13,000 years" [1]. But this book is not just a book describing past history; it attempts to explain why Eurasian civilization can finally survive and defeat other civilizations, while refuting that Eurasian hegemony is derived from Eurasian intellectuals or moral superiority. Dimon believes that the differences between rights and technology in human society cannot reflect cultural or ethnic differences, but come from environmental differences that are strongly expanded by various positive feedback cycles.

Malice: A Mystery
by Keigo Higashino


Summary: Acclaimed bestselling novelist Kunihiko Hidaka is found brutally murdered in his home on the night before he's planning to leave Japan and relocate to Vancouver. His body is found in his office, a locked room, within his locked house, by his wife and his best friend, both of whom have rock solid alibis. Or so it seems.
