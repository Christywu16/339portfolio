Welcome to My Finished Booklist

Sapiens - A Brief History of Humankind
by Yuval Noah Harari


Summary: The author tells the history of human evolution and development from the Stone Age to the 21st century, and divides human history into four stages: the cognitive revolution, the agricultural revolution, the integration and unity of mankind and the scientific revolution. The book was heavily influenced by Judd Diamond's "Guns, Germs, and Steel". Since the publication of the Hebrew edition in 2011, the book has been translated into 45 languages.

Nineteen Eighty-Four
by George Orwell


Summary: It focuses on the risk of overextension of government power, totalitarianism, and repressive rule over all people and behaviors in society. The time of the story is set in 1984-the author's imaginary vision of the future at that time.

The Silent Majority
by Xiaobo Wang


Summary: The point of view is that the author has devoted his attention to the fate of the Chinese people in this work, confronting life with irony and humor, and deconstructing the complex situation around him from a relaxed perspective.
